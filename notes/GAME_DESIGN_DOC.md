
# HACK N SLASH DECKBUILDER.


## Game Overview:
- Play on a large 2d plane, top-down, open.
- Enemies come from edges. (Enemy spawners + passive enemies - think devils-daggers)
- Player has 3 abilities: `dash, attack, special`. (Composed of a deck of cards - see more below)
- Kingshot-like setup: Squares around the board allow player to get upgrades, make turrets*, improve build.


## Abilities:
Player has a mana-bar. This constantly refills, (pretty much same as clash-royale)

Each abilities is represented by a deck of cards. eg:
Dash: 5 cards, all related to dashing/defending
Attack: 5 cards, all related to attacking
Special: 5 cards, all special-type (character-dependent)

(Cards have different costs, eg 0/1/2 mana cost.)  
When a deck is exhausted, cards get shuffed back into the ability-slot, and player can keep playing them.


## Core value prop:
hopefully, (which we will determine by testing,) player will enter a flow-state, mowing through enemies, using abilities sequentially, upgrading their build and improving their mini "base".



### Gold system:
Player will have gold stacked above their head, collected by killing enemies.  
Players spend gold by standing over "spend-points" - little rectangles that need X amount of gold to be filled.
example:
- Spend 100 gold, get a permanent perk
- Spend 30 gold, get a new card
- Spend 50 gold, remove a card
- Spend 10 gold, summon little critters to fight for you temporarily
- Spend 10 gold, get a dmg/speed buff for 20 seconds
- Spend 300 gold, create a bank-building. The Bank earns money for you passively.


## Dash/Mobility/Positioning Gameplay:
We want positioning/mobility to be very central to the gameplay.
Must give incentives for players to move around, hold positions, defend points, etc.
Knocking-back enemies and controlling the battlefield should also be important.


## Self-Balancing Gameplay:
Enemy-spawners can be killed at any time by the player.
However, killing enemy-spawners means that less enemies spawn = less gold.
So the player is kinda locked in this "equilibrium", of trying to challenge themself as much as they can.
Devils-Daggers does this and it works really well.
